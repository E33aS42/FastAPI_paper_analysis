**Summary**: This study explores the prevalence of impostor phenomenon among neurology trainees and identifies factors contributing to its peak at the commencement of neurology residency.

**Primary Focus**: The primary focus of this paper is to investigate the impostor phenomenon among junior neurologists, particularly during their early training years.

**Authors**:

1. Zhibin Tan
2. Wai Dic Foong
3. Nara Tiara Sugianto
4. Yi Rong Chiew
5. You Jiang Tan
6. Shih-Hui Lim

**Main Ideas**: 

* The prevalence of impostor phenomenon among neurology trainees is high.
* Factors contributing to the peak of impostor phenomenon at the commencement of neurology residency include lack of confidence, fear of failure, and competition from peers.
* Educators play a crucial role in helping junior doctors overcome their imposter syndrome through training programs.
