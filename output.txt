**Summary**

Impostor syndrome is a common phenomenon where individuals feel they are not good enough, despite their accomplishments. This study examines its prevalence and impact on junior neurologists in Singapore. A survey was conducted to gather data on impostor feelings, self-assessments, and coping strategies among medical students and residents.

**Primary Focus**

The primary focus of the study is to explore the experiences and attitudes of junior neurologists towards their profession, with a specific emphasis on the impact of impostor syndrome.

**Main Ideas**

The study found that 70.5% of junior neurologists reported experiencing impostor syndrome, with high levels of self-doubt and feelings of inadequacy. The most common coping strategies used by respondents were seeking feedback from others, taking on more responsibilities, and engaging in self-criticism.
